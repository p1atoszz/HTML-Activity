Editorial: Tackling Flooding in Our Town

Flooding in San Mateo has long affected families, businesses, and public spaces. In my opinion, addressing this issue requires not just infrastructure, but active community participation. The government has started projects along Marikina River, but residents can also help by properly disposing of waste and clearing waterways.

Investing in drainage improvements, flood warning systems, and green spaces can reduce risks. Community education campaigns on disaster preparedness can ensure everyone knows how to respond during heavy rains. Together, we can create a safer environment that protects livelihoods and property.

I encourage all residents to engage in local clean-up drives and attend town meetings regarding flood control. By combining government action with community effort, San Mateo can become more resilient against flooding. Let us act now to safeguard our town for the next generation.
