Flood Control Project Continues

River improvements aim to reduce flooding

The San Mateo local government continues its flood control project along the Marikina River to help prevent flooding in nearby areas. Workers have been clearing waterways and strengthening riverbanks to improve water flow during heavy rains. Officials explained that the project is part of a long-term plan to protect homes and businesses near the river.

Residents living close to the river said flooding has been a long-time concern during typhoon season. Many community members support the improvements and hope the project will reduce damage caused by strong storms. Local engineers also said the project will continue in phases over the next few years.

Public Market Renovation Begins

Better facilities planned for vendors

Renovation of the San Mateo Public Market has officially begun. The project aims to improve sanitation, lighting, and the overall layout of vendor stalls. Local officials said the renovation will create a cleaner and more organized environment for both vendors and customers.

Many residents visit the market every day to buy fresh food and household supplies. Vendors believe the improvements will attract more customers and support local businesses. The renovation is expected to be completed before the end of the year.

Tree Planting Activity in Barangay Gulod

Students help protect the environment

Students and volunteers gathered in Barangay Gulod to participate in a community tree planting activity. The program aims to increase green spaces and promote environmental awareness among residents. Organizers encouraged young people to take part in protecting the environment.

Participants planted several tree seedlings in open spaces and roadsides throughout the barangay. Community leaders said the trees will help improve air quality and provide shade in the future. The event also helped students learn about environmental responsibility.

Youth Sports Program Launched

Activities promote healthy lifestyles

The San Mateo local government launched a youth sports program to encourage young residents to stay active and healthy. The program includes sports such as basketball, volleyball, and athletics. Many students attended the opening activities and expressed excitement about joining future competitions.

Community leaders believe sports programs help young people develop teamwork and discipline. Parents also support the initiative because it provides positive activities for students after school hours. More sports events are planned in the coming months.

Free Health Check-Ups for Residents

Medical teams visit barangays

Free health check-up services were recently offered to residents in several barangays of San Mateo. Doctors and nurses provided medical consultations, blood pressure checks, and health advice. The program was organized to help residents monitor their health.

Many families attended the event to receive free medical assistance. Health workers also shared information about preventing common illnesses. The local government plans to continue offering similar services in other communities.
